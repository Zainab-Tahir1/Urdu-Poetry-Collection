Love (Ishq)

Love is a central theme in Urdu poetry, often exploring various aspects of romantic and divine love.

"Ishq par zor nahin, hai yeh woh aatish Ghalib. Jo lagaaye na lage, aur bujhaaye na bujhe."

- Mirza Ghalib

Nature

Urdu poets often use natural elements as metaphors and to set the mood in their poetry.

"Gulon mein rang bhare baad-e-naubahaar chale. Chale bhi aao ke gulshan ka kaarobaar chale."

- Faiz Ahmed Faiz

Philosophy and Mysticism

Many Urdu poems delve into philosophical questions and mystical experiences.

"Khudi ko kar buland itna ke har taqdeer se pehle. Khuda bande se khud pooche bata teri raza kya hai."

- Allama Iqbal

Social Issues

Urdu poets often address social and political issues of their time through their work.

"Aaj bazaar mein pa-bajolan chalo. Chashm-e-tar, jaan-e-shoreeda kafi nahin. Tohmat-e-ishq posheeda kafi nahin. Aaj bazaar mein pa-bajolan chalo."

- Majaz Lucknawi
