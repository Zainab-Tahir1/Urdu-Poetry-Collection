Allama Iqbal

Allama Muhammad Iqbal, widely known as Allama Iqbal, was a poet, philosopher, and politician born in 1877. He is considered one of the most important figures in Urdu literature, with literary works in both Urdu and Persian.

Iqbal is known as the "Spiritual Father of Pakistan" for his contributions to the Pakistan Movement.

خودی کو کر بلند اتنا کہ ہر تقدیر سے پہلے

خدا بندے سے خود پوچھے بتا تیری رضا کیا ہے

Mirza Ghalib

Mirza Asadullah Baig Khan, known by his pen name Ghalib, was a prominent Urdu and Persian poet during the last years of the Mughal Empire. He is considered to be one of the most popular and influential poets of the Urdu language.

Ghalib's ghazals are known for their complexity, profundity, and linguistic sophistication.

ہم کو ان سے وفا کی ہے امید

جو نہیں جانتے وفا کیا ہے

Faiz Ahmed Faiz

Faiz Ahmed Faiz was a Pakistani intellectual, revolutionary poet, and one of the most celebrated writers of the Urdu language. His work combines themes of love, beauty, and political resistance.

Faiz was nominated for the Nobel Prize in Literature and was awarded the Lenin Peace Prize by the Soviet Union in 1962.

گر ہم زندہ رہے تو کیا ہے

آنکھ جو کچھ دیکھتی ہے کیا ہے

Parveen Shakir

Parveen Shakir was a Pakistani poet, teacher, and civil servant. She is known for her use of simple language, symbolism, and exploration of concepts of love, femininity, and social stigmas.

Shakir's work marked a departure from the traditional style of women's poetry in Urdu, introducing a more modern and feminist perspective.

وہ تو خوشبو ہے، ہواؤں میں بکھر جائے گا

مسئلہ پھول کا ہے، پھول کہاں جائے گا
