Ghazal

The Ghazal is a poetic form consisting of rhyming couplets and a refrain, with each line sharing the same meter. It often expresses the pain of loss or separation and the beauty of love in spite of that pain.

  • Consists of a minimum of five couplets
  • Each couplet is independent in meaning
  • The first couplet (called Matla) sets the rhyme and refrain pattern
  • The last couplet (called Maqta) often includes the poet's pen name

ہم کو ان سے وفا کی ہے امید

جو نہیں جانتے وفا کیا ہے

- مرزا غالب

Nazm

Nazm is a major part of Urdu poetry. Unlike the ghazal, which consists of couplets not necessarily on the same theme, Nazm is a poem with a single theme from beginning to end and can be on any subject.

  • Follows a single theme or thought
  • Can be of any length
  • Not bound by rhyme or refrain rules
  • Often used for narrative or descriptive poetry

گر ہم زندہ رہے تو کیا ہے

آنکھ جو کچھ دیکھتی ہے کیا ہے

- فیض احمد فیض

Qasida

A Qasida is a form of lyric poetry that originated in Arabic literature. It is a long poem formally constructed in three parts: the nasib (an elegiac prelude), the rahil (a journey), and the main section with a panegyric, satire, or elegy.

  • Typically longer than Ghazals, often 100 or more couplets
  • Follows a single rhyme throughout the poem
  • Often used for praise, elegy, or satire
  • Requires great skill and knowledge of prosody

کیا کہیے کیا ہے کیا ہونا تھا

کچھ نہ تھا کچھ ہوا کچھ ہونا تھا

- اکبر الہ آبادی

Rubai

A Rubai is a quatrain, a four-line poem in which the first, second, and fourth lines rhyme, while the third line is different. In Urdu poetry, it's often used to express philosophical or mystical ideas in a concise form.

  • Consists of four lines (two couplets)
  • Rhyme scheme is typically AABA
  • Often expresses a complete idea or philosophical thought
  • Can be part of a longer sequence called Rubaiyat

خودی کو کر بلند اتنا کہ ہر تقدیر سے پہلے

خدا بندے سے خود پوچھے بتا تیری رضا کیا ہے

- علامہ اقبال
